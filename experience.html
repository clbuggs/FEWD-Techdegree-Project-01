Early Experiences

Middle & High School

I became a geek during middle and high school. Besides my general interest in taking things apart to find out how they worked, my school had Apple II computers in our lab, which gave me the opportunity to learn how to write simple programs.

Later I got to use computers at school which ran DOS and Windows 3.1 as well as the Macintosh. The first home computer my family owned ran Windows 95 and it was on this computer that I first experienced the world wide web.

College

I purchased my first computer, a 486sx25, during my first year of technical school. This afforded me many opportunities to learn about how computers work like upgrading the RAM, and other less fun things like troubleshooting a hard drive with bad sectors.

My first personal web page was put online at GeoCities in 1997. I taught myself HTML largely by looking at the source code of web pages I admired and with the few tutorials that were available back then.
